## The 5 Levels of Architectural Maturity
Level 1 — Static Architecture: Architecture defined as passive documentation, C4 diagrams, and static ADRs.
Level 2 — Executable Architecture: Architectural constraints continuously enforced dynamically via code, AST linters, OPA Rego policies, and pre-commit gates.
Level 3 — Observable Architecture: Architecture generates real-time telemetry, trace spans, metrics, and audit ledgers to remember its operational state.
Level 4 — Adaptive Architecture: System self-heals, hot-plugs capabilities, rotates secrets, and adjusts model routing dynamically based on telemetry signals without restarting servers.
Level 5 — Evolutionary Architecture: Closed Cybernetic Feedback Loop (Environment -> Purpose -> ... -> Evolution -> Environment) enabling controlled self-evolution across technology eras.
## Fundamental Principles
P1 — Purpose First: Business purpose drives strategy, capabilities, architecture, and implementation.
P2 — Business Before Technology: Technology serves architecture; architecture serves business.
P3 — Capability First: Enterprise organized around enduring business capabilities rather than transient tools.
P4 — Stable Core, Flexible Edge: Domain knowledge protected from infrastructure volatility.
P11 — Centennial Adaptability: Technology is epochal. Capabilities are enduring. Architecture evolves through closed-loop feedback without losing governance integrity across technology eras.
Technology serves Architecture.
